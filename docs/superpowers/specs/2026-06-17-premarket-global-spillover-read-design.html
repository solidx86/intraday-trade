US Spillover Read for the Pre-Market Briefing

Date: 2026-06-17  ·  Skill: premarket-briefing-skill  ·  Branch: feat/premarket-global-spillover-read

Approved design — implementation plan next

Problem

The Global Market Spillover section is a data dump: it lists Asia/Europe index levels with a one-clause theme each, then FX/yields lines, then one Notable overnight catalysts line. What it never does is the thing a trader actually needs at 4 a.m. ET — translate that overnight tape into "here is how it lands on US stocks at the open today." The reader is left to map index moves to US sectors in their own head.

Two concrete failures in the 2026-06-17 briefing:

  • KOSPI: N/A — no clean current close from search. CNBC's pre-markets tape page (the Step 3a dashboard) does not carry KOSPI, and the WebSearch fallback returned conflicting prints. A required index silently degraded to N/A.
  • No spillover read at all — the section ends on a raw catalysts line, with no synthesis of what the net overnight tape means for the US session.

The unlock

CNBC publishes two dedicated regional dashboards the headless browser loads cleanly: cnbc.com/markets/asia-markets/ and cnbc.com/markets/europe-markets/. They carry KOSPI (and the rest of the Asia/Europe complex) plus the per-region driver headlines — exactly the gaps the pre-markets tape leaves. Same capture recipe as the existing Step 3a tape: browser_navigate → grep the saved snapshot; the raw page never enters context.

Token discipline
Both new pages obey the established rule: browser_navigate writes the ~100k-char snapshot to a .playwright-mcp/page-*.yml file and returns only a tiny confirmation; the recipe greps the saved file for the index rows and theme headlines. Calling browser_snapshot without a filename stays forbidden.

Goals

  • Wire asia-markets + europe-markets in as supplemental browser sources behind the Step 3a tape — fill the indices the tape misses (KOSPI above all) and source the per-region themes. KOSPI stops being N/A in the normal case.
  • Add a → US Spillover Read block to Global Spillover: a sector-level synthesis that translates the overnight tape into a US-open read, organized by transmission channel.
  • Anti-fabrication, sector-level only. The read introduces no new numbers, names no watchlist tickers, fires only on material moves, and collapses to one honest line when the tape is benign — the same discipline the rest of the skill runs on.
  • Keep the 7-section structure and the per-index data lines otherwise intact — an additive synthesis layer, not a redesign.

Design

1. Two new CNBC regional pages (sources)

Added to references/data-sources.md under the Global Spillover Asia/Europe lookups, both browser-primary:

PageFills
cnbc.com/markets/asia-markets/KOSPI + any Asia index the pre-markets tape missed; Asia driver themes (BOJ/yen, China property/stimulus/tech-policy).
cnbc.com/markets/europe-markets/DAX/FTSE/STOXX confirmation + Europe driver themes (ECB/BOE, banks, energy/defense).

These are supplemental: the pre-markets page stays the primary tape (it already carries Nikkei/HSI/Shanghai/DAX/FTSE/STOXX). Route an index from the Tape Table when present; reach for these two pages for the gaps and the themes. They precede the old WebSearch fallbacks in the source order.

2. New reference — references/global-spillover-read.md

A sibling to macro-regime-read.md (same genre: a market-read framework). It defines the canonical transmission channels the read walks every run, sector-level, and the gating.

3. Gating discipline

Don't manufacture spillover from noise
A channel fires only on a material move: a regional index ≈ ≥1% on an identifiable driver, USD/JPY ≈ ≥1% or near a known intervention zone, a named overnight catalyst (central-bank decision, major data surprise, geopolitical), or a notable sector divergence within a region. Sub-threshold → the channel stays silent.
  • Benign tape → one line. When nothing clears the bar, the whole block collapses to: Overnight tape benign — no material spillover into the US open.
  • No new numbers. The read is qualitative (direction + sector). Any % it cites must already exist in the Tape Table or the asia/europe grep — it never introduces a figure.
  • Sector-level only. No watchlist tickers, no cross-reference to the 1.1 mood/regime verdict. (Both were considered and deferred — see Decisions locked.)

4. Template change — Global Market Spillover

The Asia/Europe data lines and the FX/yields lines are unchanged. A new block is appended after Notable overnight catalysts, as the section's closing synthesis:

**→ US Spillover Read (what it means at the open):**
- [Channel]: [overnight tape] — [sector effect on the US open].
- ... (only the channels that fired)
- **Net:** [tailwind / headwind / neutral overnight] — [single most-affected sector].

…or, when the tape is benign, the single fallback line. Worked example:

**→ US Spillover Read (what it means at the open):**
- China beta: HSI −0.8% on property — headwind for China-exposed semis at the open.
- Yen carry: USD/JPY 160, stable — no unwind threat to mega-cap tech.
- Europe: STOXX banks firm — mild tailwind for US financials.
- **Net:** slight chip headwind, otherwise neutral overnight.

5. Validator — one new check (schema-enforced)

  • check_spillover_read in evals/validators/validate_briefing.py: the Global Spillover body must contain a US Spillover Read marker or the no material spillover benign-fallback phrase; when the block is present it must also carry a **Net:** line. The benign-fallback line satisfies the whole check on its own.
  • Wired into run_all_checks (11 → 12 result rows); module docstring gains item 9.
  • Locks the block in the same way the 8 required indices, the mood verdict, the regime token, and the four tape lines are already locked — it can never silently drop.

6. Tests (bite-style)

New unit tests in tests/test_premarket_validator.py prove the check bites: a full block passes; a missing US Spillover Read marker fails; the benign-fallback line passes without a Net line; a block present but missing **Net:** fails; run_all_checks emits the new named result.

7. Sample data + eval rubric

Both tracked sample briefings (examples/sample-journal/daily/2026-04-06/ and .../2026-04-08/) gain a → US Spillover Read block — synthetic but plausible, consistent with each day's Asia/Europe narrative, so pytest stays green against the new check. One rubric criterion added to evals.json.
