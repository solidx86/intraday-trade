Dollar/Yields Regime Read for the Pre-Market Briefing

Date: 2026-06-16  ·  Skill: premarket-briefing-skill  ·  Branch: feat/premarket-regime-read

Approved design — ready for implementation plan

Problem

The briefing's 1.1 General Market News section ends with a single Market mood: RISK-ON / RISK-OFF verdict. That verdict is a soft, single-asset-class read — derived narratively from the day's headlines and equity-futures direction, with no cross-asset confirmation. The validator only checks that the RISK-ON/OFF token exists, not how it was derived.

That makes it the easiest signal in the briefing to fake. A green-futures headline can stamp "RISK-ON" while the bond and FX markets are still pricing fear — the classic relief-bounce that looks great at 9:30 and is dead by 11:00. The briefing currently has no structural way to catch that disagreement.

Separately, the trader has observed the DXY ↔ QQQ inverse correlation break down (e.g. overnight sessions where both rose together) and needs a rule for when "strong dollar → short QQQ" is actually tradeable.

Goals

  • Add a cross-asset regime read (dollar + 10-year yield) to section 1.1 that classifies why money is moving, grounded in two fetched data points rather than narrative.
  • Give the trader an explicit, actionable rule for when the DXY → QQQ inverse is on vs off.
  • Make the regime read a lie-detector on the mood verdict: when the two disagree, flag the divergence — it is one of the most actionable lines in the briefing.
  • Enforce the regime read durably (validator) and prove the enforcement actually bites (tests).

Background — the trading logic (one screen)

The DXY ↔ QQQ inverse is regime-dependent, not a law. It only holds when one risk driver is moving both markets. The 10-year Treasury yield (US10Y) is the tiebreaker that tells you which regime you're in:

Dollar (DXY)Yields (US10Y)RegimeWhat it meansDXY→QQQ inverse
↑↓SCAREDMoney hiding in cash and bonds — genuine fearON — short-tell works
↑↑GREEDYMoney chasing US growth / rates — not hidingOFF
↓↓GOLDILOCKSInflation fear leaving; cheap-money tailwind for techOFF
flat / mixedflat / mixedNEUTRALMove within noise of prior close, or signals conflict weaklyOFF — ignore DXY as a tell

Direction = the overnight/pre-market net move, measured as current level vs prior US cash-session close (16:00 ET) — the same "prior close" / "Friday close" line already drawn on the trader's TradingView charts. It is a snapshot at briefing time, not a forecast of the post-open trend.

Design

1. New reference file — references/macro-regime-read.md

A self-contained cheat sheet the skill loads at write time (same pattern as data-sources.md). Contents:

  • The four-regime table above (dollar × yields → SCARED / GREEDY / GOLDILOCKS / NEUTRAL).
  • The DXY → QQQ caveat: the inverse is only tradeable in SCARED.
  • The divergence / alignment matrix (below) — how the regime confirms or contradicts the mood verdict.
  • The evidenced direction discipline: an arrow may only be written from a fetched prior-close → now pair, never inferred from "the catalyst says the dollar should be up."
  • NEUTRAL definition: move within noise of prior close, or DXY/yields conflicting weakly.
Leak test
This is textbook macro — no P1/P2/P3 patterns, no parameters, no course citations — so it lives in the public skill, not the private framework supplement.

2. The divergence / alignment matrix

The two labels answer different questions (mood vs cross-asset money flow), so they can disagree. The disagreement is the signal:

VerdictRegimeWhat it really isIntraday posture
RISK-ONGOLDILOCKSReal deal — stocks ↑, dollar ↓, yields ↓Highest conviction; press strength
RISK-ONGREEDYGenuine, dollar ↑ on growth/ratesTrade longs — but inverse OFF, don't short QQQ off the strong dollar
RISK-OFFSCAREDGenuine fear — dollar ↑, yields ↓Respect it; DXY↑→short QQQ works
RISK-ONSCARED⚠️ Unconfirmed rally — stocks bid, money still in the bunkerFade-prone; size down, demand confirmation before chasing
RISK-OFFGREEDYRates-driven dip, not fear — dollar ↑ + yields ↑Don't panic-short the open; pullbacks into support tend to get bought
eitherNEUTRALCross-asset tape isn't confirming anythingTrade the equity/level read alone; ignore DXY

3. SKILL.md changes

  • Step 3b (targeted lookups) — add a fetch of DXY and US10Y as evidenced prior-close → now pairs (sibling to the existing USD/JPY fetch). Direction is what the rule needs; an exact level is optional.
  • Step 4 / Output template, section 1.1 — add two lines under the existing Market mood verdict: a Dollar/Yields regime: line (token + one-clause rationale + the evidenced pair) and a → alignment line that flags a mood/regime split when present, or states the aligned posture (firing the QQQ-inverse caveat in GREEDY/GOLDILOCKS).
  • Global Market Spillover — add DXY and US10Y as two tape lines beside the existing USD/JPY, so verified numbers sit with the other macro tape.
  • Link references/macro-regime-read.md from the relevant step (as data-sources.md is linked).

Example 1.1 output

**Market mood:** **RISK-ON** — Iran de-escalation holds; equity futures bid.
**Dollar/Yields regime:** GREEDY — DXY ↑ (Fri close 99.60 → 99.79) + US10Y ↑ (4.44% → 4.48%).
  Both rising together = money chasing US growth, not hiding — a strong dollar here is NOT risk-off.
→ Inverse is OFF today: don't short QQQ just because the dollar's green. DXY only works as a
  QQQ short-tell in the SCARED regime (DXY ↑ + yields ↓).

4. Validator — check #7 check_regime_read

  • New check in evals/validators/validate_briefing.py, scoped to the ## 1.1 General Market News body only (mirrors check_risk_verdict).
  • Passes if the body contains one of SCARED / GREEDY / GOLDILOCKS / NEUTRAL.
  • Wired into run_all_checks; module docstring + printed check-count updated (6 → 7).
  • Validated-lite by design: no regex for arrows or numbers — that would be brittle against legitimate phrasing and cause false CI failures. The evidenced-pair discipline lives in SKILL.md prose, not the validator.

5. Tests (TDD)

The existing test_premarket.py is data-driven only — it proves the corpus passes, never that a check bites. New file tests/test_premarket_validator.py closes that gap, written test-first:

  • check_regime_read passes for each of the four tokens (parametrized).
  • check_regime_read fails when no token is present (proves it bites).
  • check_regime_read is scoped to 1.1 — a token in another section does not satisfy it.
  • run_all_checks emits a result named regime_read (proves it's wired).

The existing test_briefing_passes_structural_validator automatically extends the new check across the whole tracked corpus once the sample data is updated.
