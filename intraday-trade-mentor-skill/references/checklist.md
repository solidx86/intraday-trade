# Trade Checklist — Grading Standard

Use this exact 9-point checklist for every critique. Each item is Pass or Fail. The letter grade is derived as follows:

- **A** — All 9 pass, OR 8/9 pass and the one fail is a non-fatal exit-timing/sizing imperfection.
- **B** — 7/9 pass, no fail on Q1/Q2/Q3 (the 3 Questions).
- **C** — 5–6/9 pass, OR any one of Q1/Q2/Q3 failed even if others passed (because the 3 Questions are foundational).
- **D** — 3–4/9 pass, or multiple of Q1/Q2/Q3 failed.
- **F** — ≤2/9 pass, or no plan existed at all (impromptu trade), or held past 12am MY, or overtrading rule violated.

A clean A on a losing trade is fine. A C on a winning trade is fine. The grade reflects PROCESS, never P&L.

## The 9 Points

### Q1. Trend (Daily)
- **Pass**: Daily trend matches direction taken. Long → up or sideways-in-uptrend. Short → down or sideways-in-downtrend.
- **Fail**: Trading long against a clear daily downtrend, or short against a daily uptrend, without a special-case justification (post-earnings, major catalyst, advanced failed-breakout setup).

### Q2. Pattern
- **Pass**: A real, named pattern (P1/P2/P3 + variants, Trend Continuation, valid triangle, failed breakout) was forming or formed at entry, with the candle structure supporting it.
- **Fail**: No pattern actually present — wishful pattern, "kind of looks like a P3", missing the required breakout-quality structure on a P2 (see framework), etc.

### Q3. Reward/Risk ≥ 2
- **Pass**: Distance from entry to logical PT divided by distance from entry to logical SL ≥ 2.0. Levels are technical, not arbitrary.
- **Fail**: R:R < 2, or PT/SL were not pre-defined.

### Q4. Market Sentiment Alignment
- **Pass**: For longs — QQQ/SPY/RSP not bearish or rolling over hard; VIX not spiking aggressively. For shorts — indices not ripping up; VIX not collapsing.
- **Fail**: Took the trade with indices clearly working against the direction.

### Q5. Relative Strength
- **Pass**: Ticker showing equal or stronger move than QQQ/SPY for longs (high RS), or equal/weaker for shorts (low RS).
- **Fail**: Took a long on a ticker that was underperforming a green index session, or a short on a ticker outperforming a red session.

### Q6. Entry Execution
- **Pass**: Entry at the planned level, with the required confirmation per the pattern (e.g., P2 entered on close above resistance, not on a wick; P1 entered at intraday support with rebound confirmation).
- **Fail**: Chased after a breakout candle, entered before confirmation, entered on a weak-structure setup (see framework), or entered at a worse price than the plan.

### Q7. Stop Loss Placement
- **Pass**: SL at a technical level (below intraday support / above intraday resistance), sized appropriately to the setup (tighter for a P2 breakout — see framework).
- **Fail**: SL placed by dollar amount, too wide ("safety stop"), too tight (likely to noise-stop), or no SL at all.

### Q8. Exit / Profit-Taking Discipline
- **Pass**: Exit at the planned PT, OR on a **structural invalidation** (lower-high formation, double-top at resistance, failed reclaim, distribution volume against the trade, break of intraday trendline). Cut on stop without hesitation if stopped. Manual invalidation exits before stop are **endorsed** by the methodology (cut fast on structural invalidation; wait for the next opportunity).
- **Fail**: Exited early due to **fear** (volatility/wick alone with no structural break, "felt scary"), held past PT due to greed/hope, widened stop, "just one more candle" hold, manual exit driven by emotion not technical signal.
- **Critical distinction**: An invalidation exit and a fear exit can look P&L-identical (small loss before stop), but grade oppositely. The test: can Solid name a specific structural signal? If yes → Pass. If only emotional adjectives → Fail.

### Q9. Timing Rule
- **Pass**: Trade taken within prep window through ~2 hours after open; closed before 12am Malaysia / 4am ET if intraday. No trading through a major scheduled announcement.
- **Fail**: Trade entered late in the session for the sake of having a trade; held past 12am MY; traded into Fed minutes / NFP spike without a plan.

## Quick Reference: Grade → Tone

| Grade | What it means | Drill-Sergeant Note |
|-------|---------------|---------------------|
| A | Clean execution. Loss is acceptable. | "This is the work. Lose like this 1000 times and you're consistent. Do it again." |
| B | Mostly disciplined, minor slip. | "Almost. Name the one slip and own it. Don't let it grow." |
| C | One foundational miss. | "You broke a rule. Identify which one. The rule didn't change today." |
| D | Multiple violations. | "Process collapsed. Walk back through the 3 Questions out loud. Until they're automatic, the result is luck either way." |
| F | No plan, or major rule (overtrading / overnight hold / no SL) violated. | "Stop trading. Re-screen. Re-read your trading plan. Today's lesson is that capital preservation is the only KPI that matters." |
